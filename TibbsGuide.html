The Roadmap to Your Contractor's License

Step 1

Document Your Experience

Gather proof of your work history. The state requires a combination of experience and/or education. Letters from former employers are powerful.

→
↓
Step 2

Prepare & Pass the Exam

Study for the Florida contractor examination. It covers business, finance, and trade knowledge. Many resources offer exam prep courses.

→
↓
Step 3

Submit Your Application

Complete and submit your application to the Florida DBPR. Be honest and thorough, especially regarding your history. This is critical.

Barriers to Reentry and How to Overcome Them

Top Barriers Faced

Finding stable employment and housing are often the biggest hurdles. A professional license is a powerful tool to secure a high-paying, stable career.

This chart highlights the most common challenges people face when re-entering society. Addressing the employment barrier with a skilled trade license can significantly improve outcomes.

The Power of Stable Employment

Employment Reduces Recidivism

Having a steady, well-paying job is one of the most significant factors in successfully rejoining the community and building a new future.

Individuals who secure stable employment post-release are far less likely to return to prison. Your pursuit of a contractor's license is a direct investment in a stable future.
